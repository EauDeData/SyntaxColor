Boogie Board is a paperless LCD, which uses a stainless steel telescoping stylus, or also can be drawn on with other implements, like fingers, brushes, cookie cutters, etc., as it is pressure-sensitive.
Most of the boards contain no active electronics; they are therefore as responsive as real paper.
Lifespan is estimated at 50,000 erasures.

Principle of operation[edit]
Boogie board is based on reflex display technology, i.e. LCDs that use cholesteric liquid crystal technology to reflect light in one state, and to be dark (non-reflecting) in the other state.  The dark state occurs when no voltage is applied (voltage is needed to keep the light areas of the image reflecting).

Boogie Board Sync[edit]
The Boogie Board Sync added a digitisation panel, capturing the user's input as vector graphics in PDF form for later review on a computer, phone or tablet. The board itself does not allow direct review of stored pages.

Founders[edit]
Boogie Board is developed by Kent Displays Incorporated, by researchers at

Kent State University;
Case Western Reserve; and
University of Akron; and
National Science Foundation
Market uses[edit]
It is currently used for elementary school education.
