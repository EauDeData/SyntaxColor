Annie Pokorny (born November 16, 1992)[1] is an American Cross-Country Skier, currently a member of the Sun Valley Ski Education Foundation's Gold Team.
Before finding her way to the Sun Valley Ski Education Foundation,[2] Pokorny was a student at Middlebury College in Vermont. Pokorny, in her freshman year, placed 9th in the 15K classic mass start at the 2012 NCAA Championships.
Pokorny finished in the 10k freestyle at the U.S. nationals.[3]
Her performances at the nationals earned her a spot on the U23 Team, which would take her to her first international competition. That competition was the U23 World Championships in Liberec, Czech Republic. Pokorny finished in the top 30 in two races at her first international competition.[4]
Upon her return to the United States, Pokorny won her first Eastern Intercollegiate Ski Association (EISA) in Stowe, Vermont. She mounted five podiums, helping her qualify for the NCAAs second-ranked eastern skier.
Pokorny cut her collegiate skiing career at Middlebury short and go to professional skiing. She joined the Stratton Mountain School T2 Team, training alongside notable skiers; Jessie Diggins, and Sophie Caldwell.
In 2015, Pokorny received a Travel and Training Fund Grant from The Women's Sports Foundation.[5] With those funds, Pokorny continuously trained for a spot on the US Ski Team for the 2018 Olympic Games.
